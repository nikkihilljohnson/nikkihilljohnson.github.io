Objective

Build awareness and generate qualified leads in a category most homeowners actively ignore until something breaks.

The challenge wasn't visibility. It was relevance. HVAC isn't aspirational, but comfort, safety, and a healthy home absolutely are. The goal was to reach homeowners long before urgency forced the conversation.

The Approach

  • Built a full-funnel organic and paid social ecosystem designed to earn trust first and capture demand when it surfaced
  • Established HVAC.com's social voice and paid media foundation from the ground up
  • Led with education, empathy, and homeowner-first storytelling instead of product features
  • Built awareness campaigns focused on real-life problems, not equipment specs
  • Developed retargeting pipelines to nurture high-intent audiences into qualified leads
  • Aligned organic storytelling with Meta performance campaigns to reduce acquisition friction

This wasn't about selling HVAC systems. It was about owning the conversation around home comfort.

The Insight That Drove Everything

Homeowners care deeply about their homes.

They rarely care about HVAC equipment.

The strategy pivoted from selling hardware to selling what HVAC actually represents: comfort, safety, air quality, and peace of mind.

That shift changed how people engaged with the brand and how they converted.

Content Strategy

  • Built an editorial system grounded in homeowner psychology, seasonal urgency, and everyday relatability
  • Seasonal content mapped to real homeowner stress points (summer heat, winter prep, allergy season)
  • Short-form video centered on relatable "this just happened" homeowner moments
  • Educational carousels simplifying air quality, maintenance, and energy efficiency
  • Strategic meme usage to humanize the brand and increase organic share velocity
  • Social proof styled like native community conversations to build credibility

The goal wasn't more content. It was more connection.

Paid Media Strategy

  • Structured a Meta ecosystem designed to balance education, awareness, and conversion readiness
  • Continuous creative testing across hooks, formats, and audience segments
  • Retargeting built from behavioral signals: site visitors, video viewers, engaged audiences
  • Weekly CPL and ROAS monitoring with monthly cohort optimization cycles
  • Intentional alignment between organic storytelling and paid amplification

Paid media didn't replace organic trust. It scaled it.

Performance Impact

  • $58K budget generated 1.35M+ impressions at 2.97% CTR
  • 423 qualified leads at $71 cost per lead
  • Meta campaigns attributed to ~6% of total company sales

Growth Metrics

  • 4,000% social audience growth
  • 83% increase in video views
  • 231K+ engagements across organic and paid

Not just reach. Real engagement. Real pipeline contribution.

Objective

StoneSmith didn't just need better social. They needed social. The goal was to launch from zero, build local brand recognition, and create a visual presence that showcased craftsmanship while still feeling approachable to homeowners.

The Approach

  • Built the brand's social foundation from the ground up strategically, visually, and structurally
  • Established a cohesive visual identity that reflected craftsmanship and warmth
  • Developed a content system blending education, project storytelling, and lifestyle inspiration
  • Created a consistent voice that guided homeowners from inspiration to inquiry
  • Implemented branded templates and visual standards to elevate perceived expertise
  • Built a scalable content engine designed to support future paid acquisition

This wasn't a content refresh. It was brand positioning through social.

Brand Positioning

Countertop brands tend to go one of two directions: luxury showroom vibes that feel a little out of reach, or contractor content that's technical but not inspiring.

StoneSmith needed a middle ground, beautiful work presented in a way that felt welcoming, not exclusive.

That balance became the north star for the content strategy.

Content Pillars

Built a structured system designed to balance authority, inspiration, and proof.

  • Project Showcases: Before-and-after transformations, installation process highlights, finished kitchens and bathrooms
  • Education: Material comparisons, care guides, and design trend breakdowns that simplified decision-making
  • Lifestyle: Aspirational home content placing StoneSmith within real, livable spaces
  • Social Proof: Client testimonials, reviews, and local project spotlights reinforcing credibility

Results (First 30 Days Post-Launch)

  • Consistent posting cadence established immediately
  • Cohesive visual identity implemented across platforms
  • Instagram reach increased 7.4K% organically
  • Achieved 4.2% average engagement rate (industry average: ~1-2%)
  • Strong audience response to local project storytelling

Most importantly: a scalable foundation built for future paid campaign testing and lead generation.

Objective

Transform a strong personal voice into a cohesive, visually recognizable brand, increasing followers, engagement, and short-form discoverability across platforms.

The goal wasn't to change her message. It was to package it in a way that honored both honesty and aspiration for a midlife female audience.

The Approach

  • Rebuilt the brand around clarity, consistency, and strategic repurposing
  • Centered the brand on authenticity, storytelling, and visual cohesion
  • Identified inconsistent visuals and unclear positioning as barriers to growth
  • Developed a unified look and voice that felt polished but still deeply personal
  • Repurposed podcast episodes and long-form content into high-impact short-form video
  • Built a repeatable content system to maintain consistency without creative burnout

This wasn't just a rebrand. It was a visibility upgrade.

The Problem with the Existing Brand

The voice was strong. The visuals were not.

Inconsistent colors, mixed typography, and an undefined posting rhythm created friction for new followers. The message resonated, but the presentation diluted discoverability and authority.

The brand needed alignment between substance and aesthetic.

The Rebrand Strategy

Approached the transformation methodically.

  • Conducted a full content audit to separate signal from noise
  • Defined 3-4 core content pillars aligned with audience pain points and aspirations
  • Designed a cohesive visual system (typography, color palette, graphic templates)
  • Established a consistent posting rhythm with intentional variety across pillars

The goal was clarity, not overproduction.

Short-Form Video Strategy

Her podcast was a goldmine of insight. It just wasn't being leveraged for discovery.

We transformed long-form episodes into structured short-form content designed for retention and reach.

  • Identified high-impact podcast moments with standalone value
  • Built a repeatable Reels framework: hook → insight → takeaway → subtle CTA
  • Batch-produced weekly video content to maintain consistency
  • Tested multiple hook styles to determine what stopped the scroll

Instead of creating more content, we amplified what already worked.
